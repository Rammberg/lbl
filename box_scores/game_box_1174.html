Soler Goes Deep Twice, Helps Royals Top Twins, 19-10
The Kansas City Royals powered past the Minnesota Twins, 19-10, at Target Field. Jorge Soler was 2 for 4 with 2 home runs and 2 walks. He drove in 5 runs and scored twice, to lead the charge. The win gives Kansas City a record of 17-12.

Soler had a game-changing home run in the top of the sixth. On a 2-2 slider from Trevor May, he banged a 3-run home run to right-center, giving the Royals a 11-8 lead. It was the 9th home run of the year for Soler.

"Getting a lot of baserunners and getting some timely hits, it's a good combination," said Soler.
