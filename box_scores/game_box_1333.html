Lopez Stars for Royals in 13-4 Win
Not everyone stuck through the 71-minute rain delay, but those who did went home happy.

The Kansas City Royals grabbed a win, 13-4, over the Texas Rangers at Kauffman Stadium. Kansas City second baseman Nicky Lopez went 3 for 4 with a double, 2 singles, and a walk. He scored 2 runs and drove in 3. The win lifted the Kansas City record to 21-19.

Brett Phillips had a big 3-run home run for the Royals in the bottom of the first inning. With Kansas City leading 2-0 and runners on 1st and 3rd, he got a changeup from Corey Kluber and swatted a home run to right. It was his 9th home run of the season, and more importantly, it put the Royals ahead, 5-0.

"We believe in ourselves and we're willing to work hard. It's a good combination," Lopez told the Kansas City Today.
