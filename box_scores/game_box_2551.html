Mondesi Lit, Royals Win, 6-2
The Toronto Blue Jays and Kansas City Royals delighted fans at Kauffman Stadium.

The Kansas City Royals defeated the Toronto Blue Jays, 6-2. Doing his part was shortstop Adalberto Mondesi, who went 3-4 with a home run and 2 singles, while adding 4 RBI and scoring once. Kansas City reliever Miguel Diaz was credited with the win, to go to 1-0. Jordan Romano took the loss for Toronto. Richard Lovelady was credited with the save. He has now successfully converted 9 of 12 save opportunities.

Mondesi had a game-changing home run in the bottom of the eighth. On a 2-1 fastball from Justin Miller, he banged a grand slam home run to right, giving the Royals a 6-2 lead. It was the 8th home run of the year for Mondesi.

"I thought we played a nice game," Mondesi told the Kansas City Today. "Good pitching, timely hitting, it was all there."

Kansas City, at 61-71, occupies 4th place in the American League Central Division.
