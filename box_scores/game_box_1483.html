Bannon scores 3 runs as Orioles Squeeze Past Chicago
Fans watched the Baltimore Orioles and the Chicago Cubs fight to the finish.

The Baltimore Orioles did just enough to sneak past the Chicago Cubs, 10-9, at Wrigley Field. Baltimore second baseman Rylan Bannon, in particular, had a standout performance. Mychal Givens got credit for the win, improving to 1-1. He allowed 2 runs on 3 hits in 2.2 innings.

Bannon had a game-changing home run in the top of the ninth. On a 1-1 fastball from Alec Mills, he banged a 3-run home run to left-center, giving the Orioles a 10-7 lead. It was the 3rd home run of the year for Bannon.

"Ugly win or not, we'll take it," said Bannon.

The Orioles raised their record to 18-32.
