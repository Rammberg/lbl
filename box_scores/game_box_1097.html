Astros Topple Royals, 19-4, as Altuve Shines
No one was more impactful than Jose Altuve in the 19-4 Houston win over Kansas City. Altuve, who led the AL in stolen bases with 38 in 2015, was 4 for 6 with a home run, a double, 2 singles, and a walk, while scoring 4 runs and driving in 4. Justin Verlander was the winning pitcher. Over 8 innings, he allowed 4 runs on 9 hits, with no walks and 4 strikeouts. The Astros, with just their 8th win so far in 2020, have yet to catch fire.

Houston relied on the hitting of Michael Brantley to pick up the win. The left fielder clubbed a 2-run home run in the top of the fourth inning to put the Astros on top, 4-1. For the game, Brantley had 1 home run and 2 singles in 4 at-bats.

Altuve said his wife shared good advice with him before the game. "She said, 'Don't allow this game to make you mad.' When I thought about it, I realized this is just a game. I was able to relax at the plate."
