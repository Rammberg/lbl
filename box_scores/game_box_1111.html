Altuve Excels in 9-7 Houston Win
Behind a fine performance from Jose Altuve, the Houston Astros knocked off the Kansas City Royals, 9-7. Altuve went 3-4 with a home run and 2 singles, while adding 4 RBI and scoring once for the Astros. Bryan Abreu earned the win after being called from the bullpen. The right-hander allowed no runs on 1 hit in 1.2 innings. He upped his record to 1-1. Roberto Osuna finished the game for Houston and earned his 2nd save.

Houston catcher Martin Maldonado contributed a timely at-bat in the top of the ninth. With two down, he hit a cutter into left field for a run-scoring single. It was his 14th hit of the season and put the Astros ahead, 9-7.

"You work hard, day in and day out, hoping to give the fans something to cheer about. Today was a good day," Altuve told the Houston Chronicle.

The win brings the Astros to 9-15, a soft start for a team that made the playoffs just last season.
