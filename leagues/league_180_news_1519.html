League Issues 4-Game Suspensions for Brawl
Monday, August 10th, 2020
Fabian Pertuz of the Chicago N (DSL) Cubs 2 was not happy with Yeury Tatiz, and his frustration showed when he went after the Seattle (DSL) player during yesterday's game at Baseball City Complex. Both benches cleared as they rushed to the aid of their teammates. Order was soon restored as Pertuz and Tatiz were pulled away from each other. They were ejected from the game.

Both players were suspended 4 games by the league office as a result of their tussle.

"It's something that happens in baseball every once in a while," Pertuz commented later. "We get frustrated with another player and sometimes things go too far. We'll both take our suspensions and then come back ready to play. I got no hard feelings."

In 63 games this season Pertuz has compiled a .268 batting average with 59 hits and 1 home run. He has also scored 30 times and knocked in 25 runs.

Tatiz has taken the mound for 9 relief appearances this season, nailing down no saves while notching a 2-1 record with a 5.73 ERA. The Seattle (DSL) reliever has limited opposing batters to a .312 batting average.
