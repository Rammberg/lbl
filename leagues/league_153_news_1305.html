League Suspends Cooper and Porcello for Fisticuffs
Friday, July 31st, 2020
Yesterday at Marlins Park Garrett Cooper and Rick Porcello got into each other's space and then into each other's face. They began to grapple and fell to the ground. That's when it boiled over into a full-fledged bench-clearing tussle between the Miami Marlins and New York Mets. Reason finally prevailed as the umpires restored order. After much discussion, only the two major combatants were ejected from the game.

Today the league office notified the ballclubs that Cooper will have to sit out 5 while Porcello would be given a 6-game suspension for their roles in the brawl.

This season Cooper has hit at a .340 clip with 60 RBIs, 12 home runs and 57 runs scored.

Checking out his stats for the season, Porcello has registered 3 wins and 13 losses in 25 starts, 5.66 ERA and struck out 73 batters in 111.1 innings.
