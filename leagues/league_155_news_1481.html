Romo Walks Away from the Game
Monday, August 10th, 2020
Sergio Romo is stepping away from the game of baseball at the end of the season. The Albuquerque Isotopes player gave a brief explanation to reporters this afternoon.

"It's time to step away from the game and work on phase two of my life - world domination," the 37-year-old joked.

Romo wisecracked for several minutes while reminiscing about high and low points of his career. At the end of the session, Romo sent a personal message.

"I truly thank all you fans."

Taking the hill 741 times in his career, Romo has compiled an ERA of 3.15. He has tossed 662.2 innings with 723 strikeouts and 155 free passes. Opponents have hit .218 against him. He has a record of 46-34.
