Number 2000 for Votto
Friday, September 11th, 2020
It was a great day for Joey Votto of the Cincinnati Reds -- but not a perfect day. He became the latest player to reach the 2000-hit mark, but his team lost the game. The 37-year-old first baseman got his hit in Cincinnati's 5-3 loss to San Francisco.

"It was a very special game for me, but not a good one for the team," said Votto after the game. Commenting on his achievement to reporters, he told them, "2000 hits are a lot. It puts you in a very select group. You have to hit well over a lot of seasons to do this. It's a good feeling to have your name in the record books with the other guys who have reached this level."

He finished the day with 1 hit in 4 at-bats.

All-time Votto has played in 1836 games, batted .306 with 309 home runs and 2000 base hits.
