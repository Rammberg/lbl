Salem Red Sox Are Champs
Monday, September 21st, 2020
The Salem Red Sox were good enough to get past the Myrtle Beach Pelicans at Haley Toyota Field at Salem Memorial Ballpark today and secure the 1st championship in franchise history.

"When you wear Red Sox on your shirt, you're good at something," Salem skipper Corey Wimberly told the press.

Red Sox center fielder Marcus Wilson was named MVP of the series after he went .350 with no home runs and no RBI.

"It feels totally incredible," Wilson said. "I could live to 1,000 years old and I'd remember this moment."

The new champions capped off a great season with a 6-0 victory over the Pelicans to win the Round 2 3-2.
