Peguero and Bustamante Suspended, Out 3 Games
Saturday, July 4th, 2020
Bad blood could be brewing between the Grasshoppers and the BlueClaws. In yesterday's contest at First National Bank Field, Greensboro's Liover Peguero and Lakewood's Carlos Bustamante caused the benches to empty when they went toe-to-toe against one another. Only Peguero and Bustamante were ejected by the umpires.

Upon hearing that the two players had been suspended 3 games each by the league office, harsh comments came from both clubhouses.

League officials have warned both organizations about the consequences of premeditated retaliation.

This season in 54 games Peguero is batting .244 with 1 home run, 18 RBIs and has scored 22 times.

Bustamante has made 28 relief appearances this year and notched 5 saves. To date in 30 innings he has a 2-1 record, 2.10 ERA with 24 strikeouts and 14 walks.
