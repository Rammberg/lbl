Votto Booms 300th Homer
Friday, July 24th, 2020
It was a great day for Joey Votto, but not a good one for his Cincinnati Reds. The 36-year-old first baseman reached a personal milestone with his 300th career homer, but it wasn't enough to beat the Colorado Rockies, who came away with a 6-4 victory. Votto finished the day with 1 hit, 2 RBI and 2 runs scored.

"Well, I've conceded the fact that you can't will yourself to hit a home run," he said. "I tried hard for the last few days. It was getting to be embarrassing with the crowd standing up every time I came to the plate."

Lifetime Votto has played in 1788 games with a .306 batting average. Among his 1948 hits are 420 doubles, 20 triples and 300 home runs. He also has scored 1062 runs and driven in 992 runs.
