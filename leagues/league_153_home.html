Andujar Belts 6 Hits
Monday, June 8th, 2020
It was a sensational day at the plate for Miguel Andujar as he led the charge to a 35-5 win over the Rangers. The New York third baseman was pure misery for the Texas pitching staff. He left them shell-shocked with 6 hits in 8 at-bats in an explosive display of hitting.

"Andujar put the ball into play and he really hit it hard," said Chris Woodward, manager for the Rangers, after the game. "When you do that, you've got a good chance to do some damage. And that's what happened. I feel like we threw some good pitches, but he just put some good swings on the ball."

Miguel Andujar flied out in the 2nd, singled in the 4th, hit a two-run single in the 4th, singled in the 5th, hit an RBI single in the 6th, singled in the 7th, singled in the 8th and struck out in the 9th.

Thus far this season in 40 games Andujar is batting .392 with 12 home runs, 53 RBIs and 41 runs scored. He has 71 hits in 181 at-bats.

Tampa Bay SP Morton Will Miss 5 Weeks
Monday, June 8th, 2020
Tampa Bay fans will be disappointed to learn that Charlie Morton suffered an oblique strain while throwing a pitch in today's game. After the doctor informed the Tampa Bay Rays manager that Morton was expected to miss 5 weeks, the manager told the press that the rest of the team would have to step up their play until the starting pitcher could return. "You can't make excuses in baseball, because there's always a game tomorrow," said the team's skipper.

This season Morton has a 6-2 mark in 15 starts with a 3.73 ERA. He has given up 46 hits in 62.2 innings, struck out 70 and issued 18 walks. Opposing clubs have compiled a .197 batting average against him.
