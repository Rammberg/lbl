No Comeback This Season for Shackelford
Friday, June 5th, 2020
"Not what I wanted to hear," said Texas's Kevin Shackelford to reporters this afternoon, as he revealed that the injury he sustained on 06/01/2020 would force him to miss the rest of the season. "The doctor made it clear," Shackelford said. "There's no chance for me -- none at all -- to come back this year. That's the last thing I wanted to hear." Shackelford has been out of action since he suffered radial nerve compression.

Shackelford has nailed down no saves for the year. He has also totaled 16 strikeouts and 9 walks in 19.1 innings while registering a 5.12 ERA and a 0-0 won-lost record.
Season-Ending Injury for Axford
Friday, June 5th, 2020
Obviously disappointed, John Axford confirmed today that his season is officially over. The Texas reliever offered mostly monosyllabic responses to reporters. "Nope," he said, when asked if he might possibly return this year. The 37-year-old Axford suffered a torn ulnar collateral ligament on 06/02/2020 and has been out of action since.

So far this season Axford has no saves in 16 relief appearances with a 1-1 record and 10.59 ERA.
Cabrera Gets Number 2000
Thursday, June 4th, 2020
For Melky Cabrera, it was a red-letter day. The Boston Red Sox left fielder slapped his 2000th career hit. That his team defeated the Seattle Mariners, 3-2, made it even sweeter.

His manager paid tribute to Cabrera after the game: "I've followed Melky since he came into the league," he said. "Fine ballplayer. Fine work ethic. I'm not surprised by what he's achieved."

The 35-year-old Cabrera finished the day with 1 hit in 2 at-bats.

Lifetime Cabrera has played in 1916 games, batted .287 with 150 home runs and 2000 base hits.
