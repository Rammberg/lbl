Cardinals Come up Short of Playoffs
Sunday, September 27th, 2020
The mood in the St. Louis Cardinals clubhouse was noticeably subdued today, as the club's fate on the season was officially sealed -- they will miss the postseason.

"We put ourselves in this position," said Cardinals starting pitcher Jack Flaherty. "Nobody to blame but us."

Before this season, the Cardinals last missed the playoffs in 2018. In the 145 years they have been a Major League Baseball franchise, they have missed the playoffs 123 times. In the last 10 years, they have missed the playoffs 6 times. St. Louis last won the World Series in 2011.
Cubs Win Tight National League Central Division Race
Sunday, September 27th, 2020
It's a banner year for the Chicago Cubs, they have won the National League Central Division.

"There's so much pride in our room and on our roster, anything but a division title wouldn't have been acceptable," said starting pitcher Jose Quintana. "In the playoffs we'll just have to take it one day at a time and keep battling."

The Cubs this year have battled to a 89-72 mark, which is good for the club's 24th National League Central Division title.

"I'm extremely proud of my players and look forward to seeing what we can do in the postseason," said manager David Ross.
American League East Division Champions Crowned
Friday, September 25th, 2020
The Tampa Bay Rays have taken a first big step in winning the franchise's first World Series.

"We're champions of the American League East Division and now it's time to get that championship," said catcher Victor Caratini. "The fans deserve it and all the guys in this room want to bring that title home."

It is the club's 3rd division title and although they have never won the league championship, manager Kevin Cash said the club will "certainly be prepared" once playoffs begin.
