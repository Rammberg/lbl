Longoria Belts Number 300
Wednesday, April 29th, 2020
Giants third baseman Evan Longoria reached a career milestone yesterday, blasting home run number 300.

He took the achievement in stride. "It's always good to get another home run and even better to get another win," Longoria told reporters in the postgame interview.

He went 1-for-4 during the San Francisco 4-3 triumph over the Philadelphia Phillies and scored 1 run with 1 RBI.

His career numbers show Longoria is hitting .267 with a total of 300 home runs.

Hunter Pence Calls it a Career
Wednesday, April 29th, 2020
Hunter Pence said the one thing he hopes he's remembered for in his baseball career is for being the hardest worker around.

It will be time to start reflecting on his career soon, as the 37-year-old San Francisco Giants left fielder today announced his retirement from the sport.

"I pride myself on my work ethic and I hope that rubbed off on my teammates and continues to rub off on the team after I'm gone," he said.

During his career Pence is hitting .281 with a total of 243 home runs.
Season-Ending Injury for Dolis
Wednesday, April 29th, 2020
The Toronto Blue Jays have lost the services of Rafael Dolis for the rest of this season. He suffered a torn labrum in a recent ballgame and the follow-up report from the medical staff is not good. Dolis is out for the year.
