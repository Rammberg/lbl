Hit Parade: Conforto Pops 5 Hits
Thursday, May 28th, 2020
It doesn't happen very often, but it did for Michael Conforto today -- a 5-hit effort. He went on a rampage at Citizens Bank Park and paced the Mets to a 13-4 win over the Philadelphia Phillies.

After his dynamite batting performance, the New York right fielder told reporters that he "just tried to get in a good count and have a good at-bat. That's all I can control."

His philosophy paid dividends today. He slammed 5 hits in 6 tries and put his name in the record book. Michael Conforto hit an RBI double in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, hit a solo-shot off Reggie McClain in the 5th, flied out in the 7th, singled in the 8th and hit an RBI single in the 9th.

This year Conforto is batting .283 with 5 home runs and 19 RBIs.

Veteran Cespedes Hits Three Dingers
Thursday, May 28th, 2020
The old superstition that "bad news comes in threes" certainly came true for the Philadelphia Phillies today.

New York left fielder Yoenis Cespedes tripled up on the home runs to propel the Mets to the 13-4 win at Citizens Bank Park.

"We had our chances to win the game, but we just didn't come through," Philadelphia manager Joe Girardi said. "Cespedes hit the heck out of the ball."

In addition to the homers, Cespedes collected 3 hits in 5 at-bats, 9 RBIs and scored 3 runs.

"Easy swings get you home runs," he said after the game. "I kept telling myself not to press. They have to be nice and easy swings."

Yoenis Cespedes hit a two-run home run off Jake Arrieta in the 1st, hit a three-run home run off Jake Arrieta in the 3rd, walked in the 5th, grounded out in the 7th, hit a GRAND SLAM off Victor Arano in the 8th and struck out in the 9th.

So far this year Cespedes is batting .190 with 6 home runs, 18 RBIs and 16 runs scored.
Philadelphia Loses Marrero for Season
Thursday, May 28th, 2020
"Not what I wanted to hear," said Philadelphia's Deven Marrero to reporters this afternoon, as he revealed that the injury he sustained on 05/25/2020 would force him to miss the rest of the season. "The doctor made it clear," Marrero said. "There's no chance for me -- none at all -- to come back this year. That's the last thing I wanted to hear." Marrero has been out of action since he suffered a ruptured medial collateral ligament.

For the season Marrero is currently batting .324 with 2 home runs and 18 RBIs. In 31 games he has reached base at a .381 rate and scored 14 runs to date.
