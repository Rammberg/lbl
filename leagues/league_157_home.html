Tepera Out 13 Months
Sunday, September 20th, 2020
The Tennessee Smokies have made it official: Ryan Tepera is headed to the injured list. The 32-year-old reliever has been out since 09/18/2020 with a ruptured ulnar collateral ligament. He is expected to miss 13 months.

This year Tepera has earned 2 saves, worked 32 innings, fanned 28 hitters and recorded a 3.66 ERA. He has also put up a 3-2 won-lost mark.
Round 1 Goes the Distance, Montgomery Prevails
Tuesday, September 15th, 2020
Southern League pundits who figured the Round 1 between the Montgomery Biscuits and Biloxi Shuckers would go the distance were right on the mark.

The two teams battled back and forth in a spirited series, with Montgomery coming out on top thanks to a 5-3 win in the deciding game at Montgomery Riverwalk Stadium, to wrap things up 3-2.

"You get to the last game and anything can happen, so we're overjoyed to come out on top," said Biscuits center fielder Daz Cameron, who was named series MVP.

For the series, Cameron had 7 hits for a .412 average. He had 0 home runs, 1 RBI and scored 0 runs.

The Montgomery Biscuits will play against the Tennessee Smokies in the next round. Tennessee won their series against the Jackson Generals 3 games to 0. The Round 2 schedule will be announced after the Round 1 finishes.
