League Gives Ellis and Morin 4-Game Suspensions
Tuesday, June 2nd, 2020
In an announcement today, the league informed Drew Ellis of Jackson and Mike Morin of Biloxi that they both had been suspended 4 games for fighting in a recent game.

The league president told them, "This type of aggressive behavior will not be tolerated in this league."

Ellis wasn't happy with the length of his suspension. He thought he got the worst deal and told the league commissioner, "In any fight, whoever throws the first punch should get a more severe penalty. If I threw the first punch, I'd expect to get the most punishment. Whoever took the blow should get a lesser punishment."

His manager agreed with him and said something needs to be done about it. "Ellis is right. The league needs to look at its policy and make sure the punishment fits the crime."

Ellis is swinging at a .257 pace on the year with 10 home runs, 27 RBIs and 27 runs scored. He has played in 43 games.

Morin has taken the mound for 9 relief appearances this season, nailing down no saves while notching a 0-2 record with a 4.96 ERA. The Biloxi reliever has limited opposing batters to a .284 batting average.

SL's Top Starter is Duran
Monday, June 1st, 2020
There's something about a young rookie winning the Southern League Pitcher of the Month trophy that sends shock waves through the league. Such is the case with Jhoan Duran, a 22-year-old starter for the Pensacola Blue Wahoos.

In May, he topped off the month with a 5-0 mark and 1.67 ERA in 6 starts, tossed 37.2 innings, fanned 40 and held opposing teams to a .188 average.

This season Duran has gone 6-1 with an ERA of 1.87, posting 53 strikeouts in 57.2 innings.
