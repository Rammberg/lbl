Andujar and Webb Fight, Get Suspended
Saturday, August 29th, 2020
Miguel Andujar of the New York Yankees and Tyler Webb of the Cleveland Indians will be on the sidelines for a few days. They have been suspended for 4 and 9 games respectively by the league office for causing the bench-emptying free-for-all at the recent Yankees-Indians game at Yankee Stadium.

Neither player had any comment about the suspensions. Both clubs told BNN Andujar and Webb will begin serving them soon.

So far this season Andujar has put up a .336 batting average with 145 hits, 29 home runs and 111 RBIs.

Webb has taken the mound for 51 relief appearances this season, nailing down 2 saves while notching a 2-2 record with a 9.91 ERA. The Cleveland reliever has limited opposing batters to a .295 batting average.
