Injury Shuts Down Perez for Season
Friday, May 15th, 2020
Cionel Perez had hoped for the best, but expected the worst. Today, he learned from the Round Rock medical staff that he would miss the rest of the season. The Round Rock starting pitcher suffered a torn ulnar collateral ligament in an earlier ballgame. He admitted to being discouraged, but said he plans to come back strong next year.

Up to now Perez has recorded a record of 2-2. In 6 starts he has fanned 18 batters in 29 innings with a 4.66 ERA.
Cuevas and Knapp Brawl, League Suspends Them
Thursday, May 14th, 2020
Noel Cuevas of the Iowa Cubs and Ricky Knapp of the Oklahoma City Dodgers received notification from the league commissioner's office that they had been suspended for 4 and 8 games respectively for their part in the on-field ruckus in a recent game at Principal Park.

Cuevas currently has a .247 batting average with 22 hits, 2 homers, 11 RBIs, 14 runs scored and a .330 OBP.

Knapp has taken the mound for 9 relief appearances this season, nailing down no saves while notching a 0-1 record with a 4.50 ERA. The Oklahoma City reliever has limited opposing batters to a .273 batting average.
Reno Southpaw Tosses 5-0 Shutout
Tuesday, May 12th, 2020
The Reno Aces rolled to an easy 5-0 win over the Tacoma Rainiers at Cheney Stadium today. Leading the way to victory was starter Dillon Overton, who shackled the Tacoma Rainiers and held them scoreless on a 3-hitter.

"This is what every pitcher wants to do when they go out there," Overton said in his postgame press conference.

Overton has taken the mound for 5 relief appearances this season, nailing down no saves while notching a 1-1 record with a 4.24 ERA. The Reno reliever has limited opposing batters to a .262 batting average.
