Dubon Belts 5 Hits
Thursday, July 2nd, 2020
It was a good day for the River Cats at Las Vegas Ballpark as they clipped the Las Vegas Aviators, 10-7. It was tremendous day for Mauricio Dubon, who was on a hitting barrage at the plate, finishing 5-for-5.

"I don't know what to call that," the Sacramento second baseman told reporters after his big performance. "I'm not 'dialed in' or 'in a zone,' I just try to keep it simple. Basically the pitcher throws the ball, I try to pick up the spin, then I hit it. Today I picked up the spin pretty well."

Mauricio Dubon singled in the 1st, hit a two-run double in the 2nd, hit a solo-shot off Stephen Johnson in the 4th, singled in the 6th, singled in the 7th and was hit by a pitch in the 9th.

Currently Dubon is hitting .331 and has collected 7 home runs, 41 RBIs and scored 32 times, while playing in 66 games.
PC Announces June's Best Starter
Wednesday, July 1st, 2020
There's something about a young rookie winning the Pacific Conference Pitcher of the Month trophy that sends shock waves through the league. Such is the case with Dillon Peters, a 27-year-old starter for the Salt Lake Bees.

In June, he topped off the month with a 5-0 mark and 2.20 ERA in 5 starts, tossed 32.2 innings, fanned 26 and held opposing teams to a .221 average.

On the season Peters has 10 wins and no losses and a 2.95 ERA.
Lee Acclaimed Top Pitcher in June
Wednesday, July 1st, 2020
Any time a rookie makes a splash on the scene, it's got to be great for his career potential. Just ask Chris Lee, who put together a stellar month out of the pen. The San Antonio Missions reliever, 27, kept the opposition off balance all month as he held them to a .238 average and walked away with the American Conference Pitcher of the Month award for June.

Lee posted a 3-0 mark and a 0.79 ERA in 10 relief appearances. In 11.1 innings, he had no saves, 14 strikeouts, and 3 walks.

To date, Lee has collected no saves in 13 relief appearances. He has rung up 17 strikeouts and 6 walks in 14.1 innings while registering a 1.26 ERA and a 3-1 won-lost record.
