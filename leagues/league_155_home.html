PCL News: The Offseason Begins
Wednesday, September 23rd, 2020
It was the best of times in the just-finished PCL season. It is now the offseason in baseball. While the annual awards will be announced shortly, for many fans and some players, this is the worst of times. An old-time player was once asked what he did in the offseason. He replied, "Mostly, I just stand at the window and stare and wait for the new season." Many fans feel that way, too.

The feeling is certainly different in PCL front offices, though. For those men and women, the offseason is a hectic time of roster evaluations, analysis and change. Who will re-sign? What players will we go after? Where should we send our scouts? Who will be hanging up his spikes? Each club's success for next year and beyond will be largely determined by the wheelin' and dealin' that transpires over the next several months.

Tacoma Triumphs in Round 2
Monday, September 21st, 2020
It's party time in Tacoma. The confetti is flying and residents are smiling.

The Tacoma Rainiers have reached the mountaintop, capturing the 2020 Round 2 today. They defeated the San Antonio Missions 2-1 at Cheney Stadium to officially wrap up the series 3-1.

Asked how it feels to be champion, catcher Brian O'Keefe was to the point.

"It feels great, like a million bucks. I'm so happy right now, not just for me but for all the guys," he said. "We put a lot of work into this and deserve to be champions."

The team is planning a victory parade and fan appreciation day later in the week. Tacoma finished the regular season 81-59 and claimed first place in the Pacific Conference North Division.
