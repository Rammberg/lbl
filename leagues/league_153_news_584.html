Alvarez Hits 3 Homers Against Texas
Tuesday, June 9th, 2020
Houston Astros first baseman Yordan Alvarez put on a dazzling display of power today at Minute Maid Park against the Texas Rangers. In the 28-9 Astros victory Alvarez penned his name into the record books, slamming a trio of home runs in one of baseball's finest single-day efforts.

"He can hit them out anywhere, right, center, left, it doesn't matter," Texas manager Chris Woodward said. "He's super strong and stays on the ball so well."

Along with his three home runs, the 22-year-old had 4 hits in 6 at-bats, 7 RBIs and 6 runs scored.

"Life will always throw you curves, just keep fouling them off," Alvarez said after the game, when asked about his hitting philosophy. "The right pitch will come, and when it does, be prepared to run the bases. Or trot around them, like I did today."

Yordan Alvarez hit a solo-shot off Lance Lynn in the 1st, hit a solo-shot off Rafael Montero in the 3rd, hit a two-run double in the 3rd, hit a three-run home run off Matt Duffy in the 4th, walked in the 5th, grounded out in the 6th and struck out in the 7th.

Alvarez on the season is hitting .369 with 20 home runs. His stats include 70 RBIs and 54 runs scored.
