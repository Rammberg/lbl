TEX News: The Offseason Begins
Sunday, September 20th, 2020
It was the best of times in the just-finished TEX season. It is now the offseason in baseball. While the annual awards will be announced shortly, for many fans and some players, this is the worst of times. An old-time player was once asked what he did in the offseason. He replied, "Mostly, I just stand at the window and stare and wait for the new season." Many fans feel that way, too.

The feeling is certainly different in TEX front offices, though. For those men and women, the offseason is a hectic time of roster evaluations, analysis and change. Who will re-sign? What players will we go after? Where should we send our scouts? Who will be hanging up his spikes? Each club's success for next year and beyond will be largely determined by the wheelin' and dealin' that transpires over the next several months.

Tulsa Sweeps Round 2
Friday, September 18th, 2020
The Tulsa Drillers rolled over the Midland RockHounds 4-1 at ONEOK Field to easily win the 2020 Round 2 crown.

Tulsa won in a walk and swept the series from the RockHounds 3-0. It was their 1st championship title.

Winning manager Scott Hennessey praised his own club as well as the opposition, calling the RockHounds "a fine team."

"It wasn't as easy as it looked. We had to play good baseball to beat them," he said. "We always seemed to come up with the key hit at the right time and to make the plays on defense when we had to. That's how you win the Round 2."

The Drillers took first place in the North Division and rounded out the year with a 79-61 record.
