CAL News: The Offseason Begins
Wednesday, September 23rd, 2020
Wait until next year!
That is the battle cry for the fans of all but one CAL team. While the 2020 champs get to brag to their buddies about the championship, it's time for general managers to rehash last year and figure out ways to improve their teams. It is the offseason in baseball. The diamonds and the stadiums are void of activity, but the front offices are bristling with energy. Good teams want to stay good -- bad teams want to get good -- and all teams want to get better.

So get ready for the ecstasy of a superstar savior's signing, the joy of re-signing a valuable performer, but also the heartbreak of losing a bidding war or saying farewell to that retiring vet. Nothing is happening on the field, but off the field, the offseason is rarely dull.

Lake Elsinore Triumphs in Round 2
Monday, September 21st, 2020
Sometimes the winner is the one who got the lucky bounces and the lucky calls, and sometimes the winner is just the one who played better.

It was the latter case in 2020, as the Lake Elsinore Storm topped the Stockton Ports 5-4 tonight at Lake Elsinore Diamond to win the Round 2. It was the 1st title in franchise history.

"When you play for championships, you have to play better than the team in the other dugout. We did that in this series, and all year. Now we're the best team in baseball," said Lake Elsinore first baseman Hudson Potts, who was named series MVP.

The Storm took the series 3-1. The new titleholders completed the regular season with a second-place finish in the South Division and a 70-71 record.

Lake Elsinore manager Mike McCoy grinned and nodded when asked to comment on Potts's words.

"Hudson said it best -- we played better than the other team," he said.

Hometown officials announced they were already planning a "grand celebration" for the winners in the days to come.
