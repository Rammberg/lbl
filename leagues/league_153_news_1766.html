O'Neill Gets 5 Hits
Wednesday, August 26th, 2020
After a 5-hit effort to lead St. Louis's 35-33 victory over the Cincinnati Reds, right fielder Tyler O'Neill told BNN beat writers it had been "a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "Three or four is better, but five or more is out of sight. And to do that at the Major League Baseball-level is really something special."

The 25-year-old player earned his accolades today at Great American Ball Park with a spectacular 5-for-6 game against the Reds.

"His swing is getting there," said Cardinals skipper Mike Shildt, who spoke glowingly of O'Neill after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Tyler did that to perfection today."

Tyler O'Neill walked in the 1st, walked in the 2nd, hit an RBI single in the 4th, hit an RBI single in the 5th, struck out in the 7th, hit a GRAND SLAM off Raisel Iglesias in the 7th, hit a two-run home run off Aristides Aquino in the 8th, walked in the 9th and hit an RBI single in the 10th.

O'Neill is hitting at a .246 mark this season. His stats show 21 home runs, 78 RBIs and 51 runs scored. He has played 96 games for the Cardinals.
