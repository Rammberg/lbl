Trout Rips HR Number 300
Saturday, June 27th, 2020
Toronto defeated Los Angeles today 9-5 and took some of the joy out of Mike Trout's 300th career home run.

"I just wanted to get a good swing on the ball all day," Trout said. "And I did."

Despite the loss, it was a good game for the 28-year-old Angels center fielder. He had 2 hits, scored 1 run and had 2 RBI.

The Blue Jays manager said, "You could see he was aggressive up there. The worst thing we did was throw him a strike. It probably would have been better if we threw the ball outside of the strike zone every time he came to the plate because he seemed like he was in a hurry to get it over with."

Trout has a lifetime .303 batting average and .419 on-base percentage in his 1270-game career with 1391 hits and 300 home runs.
