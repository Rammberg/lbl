Suspensions Handed Out for Bench-Clearing Brawl
Saturday, June 20th, 2020
After an intense confrontation, both the White Sox Jose Abreu and the Indians Scott Moss were handed their punishments today. The league commissioner's office issued suspensions for an on-the-field conflict that occurred at a recent Chicago-Cleveland game. Jose Abreu will miss 5 games, while Scott Moss was suspended for 6 games.

"Sometimes we go too far and have to pay the consequences," Jose Abreu commented later. "Hopefully cooler heads will prevail the next time we play."

Looking at his stats this year, Abreu has compiled a .252 batting average, while hitting 6 home runs and driving in 27 runs. He has played in 66 games and scored 28 runs.

In 2 starts this season Moss has gathered no wins while suffering 1 loss. He has pitched 15 innings, allowed 17 hits, struck out 19 and has a 3.00 ERA.
