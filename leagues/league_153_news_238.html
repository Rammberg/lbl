Cooper's Strong Week Earns NL Trophy
Monday, May 11th, 2020
At the age of 29, Garrett Cooper might have some potential he hasn't realized yet -- if his success at the plate last week is any indication. The Miami first baseman put up some exceptional numbers the past seven days with a .543 batting average. For his efforts Cooper collected the National League Player of the Week Award.

He racked up 19 hits in 35 at-bats, 1 home run and 13 RBIs last week in 10 games.

This season Cooper has delivered 29 hits in 62 at-bats for a .468 average with 2 home runs. He is getting on base at a .515 clip and his totals also include 17 RBIs and 12 runs scored. This year Cooper has played in 17 games.
