Adrianza, Wood Receive 3-Game Suspensions
Thursday, September 10th, 2020
The fans at Target Field saw it all yesterday in the Twins-Indians game -- pushes, shoves, scuffles, a few tackles, a few swings, a few feeble punches -- and a baseball game, too. When the ruckus was over, only two players were sent to the showers by the umpire crew. Minnesota's Ehire Adrianza and Cleveland's Hunter Wood were ejected from the game.

Today the two players were suspended 3 games by the league commissioner.

This year Adrianza is hitting .293 with 6 home runs and 36 RBIs to his credit. He has gotten on base at a .332 clip and scored 27 runs.

Wood has taken the mound for 47 relief appearances this season, nailing down no saves while notching a 4-1 record with a 5.31 ERA. The Cleveland reliever has limited opposing batters to a .289 batting average.
