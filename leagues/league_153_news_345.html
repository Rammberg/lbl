Nationals End Seager's Streak
Tuesday, May 19th, 2020
It was the best of both worlds for the Washington Nationals today at Nationals Park, as they defeated the Seattle Mariners 4-3 and also snapped Kyle Seager's 20-game hitting streak. The 32-year-old Seattle third baseman failed to get a hit in 3 at-bats.

"Nothing personal against Seager, we just wanted to win the game," said Nationals skipper Dave Martinez. "We did a great job keeping him off-balance and guessing."

When asked by reporters about his streak coming to an end, Seager frowned and said, "I won't lie and say that I'm not disappointed about it, but I'd feel much better if we had won."

So far in 2020 Seager is batting .351 with 13 home runs, 39 RBIs and 22 runs scored. He has 47 hits in 134 at-bats and a .408 OBP.
