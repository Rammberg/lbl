Trouble in Philadelphia, Gregorius Suffers Setback
Tuesday, June 16th, 2020
"It's worse than we thought." That was the news from the Phillies clubhouse today as it was learned that shortstop Didi Gregorius will be out for an added 2 weeks as a result of a recent injury. His treatment for a strained rib cage muscle will have to be extended. Initially it didn't seem the injury was a major concern, but the Philadelphia shortstop told team trainers of some discomfort yesterday morning, and upon examination it was determined to be a more serious injury. "We'd rather take this thing slowly and make sure Didi gets back onto the field healthy," said the team's head trainer.

This season Gregorius is batting .287 with 69 hits, 15 home runs, 47 RBIs, 14 walks, .323 OBP and has scored 37 runs. He has played in 60 games.
