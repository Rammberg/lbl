Ward Goes 5-for-6
Thursday, May 21st, 2020
Today at NYSEG Stadium, Drew Ward put on an impressive display.

The Harrisburg Senators first baseman tagged Binghamton pitching for 5 hits in 6 at-bats, but his teammates were unable to capitalize on the spree and lost the game to the Binghamton Rumble Ponies by the score of 8-7.

"The loss takes some of the joy out of it, but I'll take it," Ward said. "5-for-6 ain't bad in any league."

Despite the terrific performance, the Senators manager was unwilling to offer his first baseman much praise, saying he holds his "whole roster" accountable for losses.

"Drew told me 'Coach, I had 5 hits' and I said 'Well that's great, but it looks like we needed one more from you,'" manager Billy Gardner Jr. said.

Drew Ward singled in the 1st, singled in the 2nd, singled in the 5th, struck out in the 7th, hit an RBI double in the 9th and hit a solo-shot off Nick Rumbelow in the 11th.

Currently Ward has 3 home runs and a .321 batting average.
