APP Most Valuable Player Award Goes To Henry
Monday, September 21st, 2020
At the age of 21, Isaiah Henry of the Burlington Royals could very well have his best years ahead of him.

It's a startling thought when you consider the year he had in 2020 was good enough to win the right fielder the Appalachian League Most Valuable Player Award.

"See the ball, hit the ball," he said. "That's my basic approach."

The top hitter put up a .321 batting average this year, as well as a .391 on-base percentage. He played 65 games and racked up 80 hits, 12 doubles, 3 triples, 8 home runs, 47 RBIs and 45 runs scored.

He received 5 first place votes out of a possible 10. Alex Toral of the Greeneville Reds finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Mitch Spence of the Pulaski Yankees finished third with 3 first place votes.
