Big 3-HR Day for Alvarez
Friday, June 26th, 2020
Houston Astros first baseman Yordan Alvarez put on a dazzling display of power today at Minute Maid Park against the Texas Rangers. In the 29-5 Astros victory Alvarez penned his name into the record books, slamming a trio of home runs in one of baseball's finest single-day efforts.

"He can hit them out anywhere, right, center, left, it doesn't matter," Texas manager Chris Woodward said. "He's super strong and stays on the ball so well."

Along with his three home runs, the 22-year-old had 3 hits in 6 at-bats, 10 RBIs and 3 runs scored.

"Life will always throw you curves, just keep fouling them off," Alvarez said after the game, when asked about his hitting philosophy. "The right pitch will come, and when it does, be prepared to run the bases. Or trot around them, like I did today."

Yordan Alvarez hit a two-run home run off Arturo Reyes in the 1st, struck out in the 2nd, grounded out in the 4th, hit a three-run home run off Josh Fields in the 4th, walked in a run in the 5th, struck out in the 6th and hit a GRAND SLAM off Matt Duffy in the 8th.

In this current campaign Alvarez is hitting at a .356 clip with 29 home runs and 105 RBIs.
