APP Hands Out Reliever of the Year
Thursday, September 17th, 2020
Adam Lukas was a calming influence out of the Burlington Royals bullpen this season.

His teammates and manager said he had a poised demeanor about him that was typical of someone much older and more experienced. His poise has paid off, as the 22-year-old turned in a sparkling season and has been named the winner of the Appalachian League Reliever of the Year for 2020.

The Grafton, Wisconsin native won the award with 7 saves in 23 relief appearances, 42 strikeouts in 38.2 innings and a record of 3-2 to go with his 1.86 ERA.

He received 6 first place votes out of a possible 10. Nick Fraze of the Bluefield Blue Jays finished second in voting with 4 first place votes, while Cam Cowan of the Bluefield Blue Jays finished third.
