Padres Chill Turner's Hot Streak
Friday, August 21st, 2020
It was the best of both worlds for the San Diego Padres today at Petco Park, as they defeated the Washington Nationals 10-1 and also snapped Trea Turner's 24-game hitting streak. The 27-year-old Washington shortstop failed to get a hit in 1 at-bat.

"Nothing personal against Turner, we just wanted to win the game," said Padres skipper Jayce Tingler. "We did a great job keeping him off-balance and guessing."

When asked by reporters about his streak coming to an end, Turner frowned and said, "I won't lie and say that I'm not disappointed about it, but I'd feel much better if we had won."

For the season Turner has produced 152 hits in 467 at-bats for a .325 average with 16 home runs and gotten on base at a .386 clip.
