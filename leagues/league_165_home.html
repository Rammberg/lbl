NWL News: The Offseason Begins
Thursday, September 17th, 2020
Wait until next year!
That is the battle cry for the fans of all but one NWL team. While the 2020 champs get to brag to their buddies about the championship, it's time for general managers to rehash last year and figure out ways to improve their teams. It is the offseason in baseball. The diamonds and the stadiums are void of activity, but the front offices are bristling with energy. Good teams want to stay good -- bad teams want to get good -- and all teams want to get better.

So get ready for the ecstasy of a superstar savior's signing, the joy of re-signing a valuable performer, but also the heartbreak of losing a bidding war or saying farewell to that retiring vet. Nothing is happening on the field, but off the field, the offseason is rarely dull.

Sweet Sweep for Tri-City
Tuesday, September 15th, 2020
With a 4-2 win over the Eugene Emeralds tonight at Gesa Stadium, the Tri-City Dust Devils wrapped up their 1st title in team history.

Tri-City left little doubt who was the better club. "Team" was the word of the day as the Dust Devils celebrated the culmination of their banner year in the victors clubhouse following the game.

"Hitting, pitching, fielding; those are all things you need to win, but most importantly you need a team, a team that works together toward the same collective goal," said Tri-City manager Vinny Lopez. "Our players felt that way all year and they played that way. When one guy had an off day, 24 others stepped up their game and filled the void."

A jovial mood filled the air as Tri-City celebrated into the night. The city's mayor said parade plans were underway and a "huge celebration" in the works.

"We're going to celebrate our team, our championship Tri-City Dust Devils," he said.

The champions completed the regular season with a first-place finish in the North Division and a 37-39 record.
