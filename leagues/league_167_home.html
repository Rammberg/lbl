No Easy Feat: Pitcher Wins AZL Most Valuable Player Award
Wednesday, September 23rd, 2020
Fans of the Chicago N (AZL) Cubs Blue are hoping there are many more awards to come for their talented ace, Jackson Rees.

At the age of only 26, the star pitcher has been named the Arizona Rookie League Most Valuable Player Award winner for 2020.

"We knew Rees was a special player, but I don't think anyone expected him to get this good, this fast," the Chicago N (AZL) manager said.

Rees's dominant season saw him throw 64 innings, with a 1.27 ERA and .206 opponent's batting average. He had a record of 8-1 through 10 starts and compiled a 1.6 WAR along the way.

He received 8 first place votes out of a possible 21. Michael Martinez of the Los Angeles N (AZL) Dodgers finished second in voting with 5 first place votes, while John Rodriguez of the Los Angeles N (AZL) Dodgers finished third.

Rees Takes Home AZL Outstanding Pitcher Award
Tuesday, September 22nd, 2020
The Arizona Rookie League announced today that Jackson Rees has been chosen the Outstanding Pitcher Award winner for 2020. The Chicago N (AZL) starter had everything working for him this past season as he breezed to a 8-1 record.

In his award-winning season Rees crafted a 1.27 ERA in 64 innings with 43 strikeouts and 17 walks. Opposing batters hit .206 against him in his 10 starts.

He received 15 first place votes out of a possible 21. Dawson Merryman of the Chicago N (AZL) Cubs Blue finished second in voting with 6 first place votes, while Tanner Andrews of the Milwaukee (AZL) Brewers Gold finished third.

Arizona Rookie League Announces Best Manager
Monday, September 21st, 2020
Nick Stanley stepped before the microphone today and accepted his Arizona Rookie League Manager of the Year award, thanking the whole Milwaukee (AZL) Brewers Gold organization for making the 2020 season such a success.

Stanley struggled to find the right words. "My father was a drill instructor in the military. He taught me that you have to work hard, set goals and accept responsibility for your actions. That's what the entire Milwaukee (AZL) organization did this year. My dedicated owner, coaching staff, hard-working and talented players and loyal front office all made this award possible. I thank them for it."

And what a year it was. Stanley sparked the Brewers Gold to a 38-18 record and a first in the Central Division standings.
