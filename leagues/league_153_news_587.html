Pirates Spank Giants, 9-0
Wednesday, June 10th, 2020
Today was Joe Musgrove's turn in the rotation for the Pittsburgh Pirates and he made the most of it, shutting out the San Francisco Giants on 5 hits.

The 27-year-old right-hander led the Pirates to an easy 9-0 victory, allowing no walks and tallying 6 strikeouts.

"I hope we miss him next time," San Francisco manager Gabe Kapler said. "Musgrove certainly pitched a strong game against us today. He was in a groove, throwing everything for strikes. Throwing hard. Changing speeds."

On the season Musgrove has compiled a 4-2 mark with a 1.95 ERA in 15 starts. In pitching 69.1 innings he has 65 strikeouts and 10 walks while limiting the opposition to a .191 batting average.
