Jurickson Profar Bashes Three over the Wall
Thursday, July 2nd, 2020
It was a good day for the San Diego Padres and an exceptional one for Jurickson Profar. The 27-year-old second baseman sent three bombs out of the park to help the Padres beat the Philadelphia Phillies 14-3 at Citizens Bank Park.

"When you're in the zone, the game really slows down," Profar said when asked about his performance later. "I couldn't even tell you what pitches were thrown. Today feels great, but it is a long season and tomorrow is another day."

For the day, he scored 4 times, drove in 4 runs and smacked 4 hits in 6 at-bats.

Jurickson Profar flied out in the 1st, hit a solo-shot off Jake Arrieta in the 3rd, grounded out in the 4th, hit a two-run home run off Trevor Bettencourt in the 6th, singled in the 8th and hit a solo-shot off Victor Arano in the 9th.

This season Profar has hit at a .286 clip with 52 RBIs, 11 home runs and 39 runs scored.
