Murdock Hurls No-Hitter
Thursday, July 16th, 2020
Noah Murdock really did a number on the Greeneville Reds at Pioneer Park today -- and that number was zero.

The Burlington Royals hurler had what he later called "one of the best moments of my life," tossing a no-hitter and leading the Royals to victory, 6-0.

"This is something special," Murdock said. "I knew from the fifth inning that I had a no-hitter working. I tried to get it out of my mind, but you just can't. They have too many scoreboards in the park. They're everywhere you look."

The right-hander faced only 29 batters in the no-hit gem, struck out 6 and walked 1.

"We simply couldn't get any wood on the ball," Greeneville manager Derrin Ebert said when asked about the epic performance. "Murdock was brilliant out there. He certainly had our number."

Looking at his season stats, Murdock has won 1 and lost 1, collecting 19 strikeouts and 8 walks in 28 innings with a 1.93 ERA.
