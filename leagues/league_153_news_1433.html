Veteran Encarnacion Hits Three Dingers
Tuesday, August 4th, 2020
Edwin Encarnacion went yard three times in a Boston Red Sox victory over the Baltimore Orioles at Oriole Park at Camden Yards today.

"I was in a groove," he said. "Every pitch they tossed up there looked like a grapefruit."

For the day, the Boston first baseman went 3-for-3 against Baltimore with 4 RBIs and 5 runs scored in a brilliant display of power hitting that led the Red Sox to the 15-6 win.

"I tell you what. I'm shocked because I didn't think that last ball had enough to get out," he told BNN reporters in the clubhouse following the game. "I thought it was just going to be a long out."

Edwin Encarnacion walked in the 1st, hit a solo-shot off Dean Kremer in the 3rd, hit a two-run home run off Dean Kremer in the 5th, walked in the 6th, hit a solo-shot off Jim Johnson in the 8th and walked in the 9th.

This season Encarnacion is batting .294 with 76 RBIs, 95 runs scored and 36 home runs.
