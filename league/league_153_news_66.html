Bryce Harper Drills 3 HR in One Game
Wednesday, April 22nd, 2020
Philadelphia Phillies right fielder Bryce Harper had a magnificent game against the Texas Rangers today, rocketing three balls out of Citizens Bank Park to help the Phillies take the 13-8 victory.

"I got some good pitches to hit and I creamed them," he said after the game. "The first was a big, fat fastball and the last two were hanging curves. All three were mistakes."

For the game, Harper went 3-for-3 with 4 RBIs and 3 runs scored.

"There's a fine line between hitting home runs and line drives," Philadelphia manager Joe Girardi said later. "Seems like Bryce found his home run stroke today."

Bryce Harper hit a solo-shot off Jordan Lyles in the 2nd, hit a solo-shot off Jordan Lyles in the 4th, walked in the 4th, walked in the 6th and hit a two-run home run off Josh Fields in the 8th.

Harper is presently batting .385 with 4 home runs, 4 runs scored and 8 RBIs. His OBP is .467.
